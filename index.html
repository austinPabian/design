Michigan Association of School Boards

BRIEF

During my time at the Michigan Association of School Boards, I encountered a wealth of branding materials that, while abundant, lacked cohesion and a clear direction. I took the opportunity to bring consistency and purpose to the organization’s brand. From creating a formal brand book and redesigning essential documents to leading social media campaigns and rebranding major publications, I helped shape a unified and professional identity. What you see here represents just a small piece of the impact I made.

Brand Book

Ideation, conception, design, and layout of all components for MASB's brand. When I came to MASB, the brand book provided was more of a templated, "this is what your logo and colors look like" outline rather than something that was set up to evolve with the brand. I decided to do change that.

Annual Conference Print Mailer

This was designed to build (pun intended) on the blueprint theme for MASB's 2018 Annual Leadership Conference, resulting in what ended up being a multi-fold, semi-perforated, USPS-approved mailer. I came up with this idea to break away from the typical four-page letter-sized print mailer, and developed several iterations before finalizing the folds with our local printer. Developing the visuals and messaging structure, I ensured that a recipient opening it up would experience what can only be described as an MASB cootie catcher.

Beggar's Banquet

BRIEF

The owners approached me through a mutual contact to revise their decades-in-the-making menu, as flipping through page over page wasn't working for many patrons. In our discovery, we outlined two vital components: it had to be high in contrast (due to dim restaurant lighting), and it had to be something they could easily have new prints made of, as the menu changes often. My solution to this was a bifold tabloid-sized menu with everything plainly spelled out.

Save Our Stamps

BRIEF

The COVID-19 pandemic indavertently saw the USPS come under public scrutiny. Knowing I couldn't personally fund my local post office or give thanks to every postal worker in the region due to social distancing, I did the next best thing and launched a campaign to educate and fundraise by way of custom, limited-run apparel. I developed, marketed, and conducted constant outreach to gain momentum for the cause, and at the end of all of it, I donated 100% of the profits generated by the sales to local businesses in the form of prepaid postage. How's that for a two-fer?
Everything here from creative direction to development and execution by me.

Logo and Artwork

Custom logo and artwork paying subtle homage to the USPS branding. The truck art features the iconic Grumman LLV mail truck silhouette with additional branding components used throughout the campaign.

Michigan Association of School Boards

BRIEF

While at MASB, we underwent a full website redesign and migration to a new platform. As the graphic designer, I was responsible for upholding the visual layout and refining the general look and feel of the site. Working between our internal team and the vendor, I developed this site mockup with additional functioning components to keep the project on track. In addition to this, I single-handedly translated these styles and visual presence for a simultaneously-updated association management system. Unfortunately, that portal is restricted to a member login, but you get the gist.

Site Mockups and Development of Desktop and Mobile UX/UI

Striking a visual balance between conveying accessible information while retaining the breadth of opportunity an organization founded in 1949 has to offer was no simple feat. We researched and refined our approach to ensure school board members would find the new site refreshing and modern while retaining the familarity and helpful functionality they've come to know.
