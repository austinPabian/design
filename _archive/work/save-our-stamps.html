SAVE OUR STAMPS

BRANDING + CAMPAIGN

While the world was in turmoil as the COVID-19 pandemic struck, a spotlight grew on the United States Postal Service. Scrutiny over delayed and lost mail (especially during an election year) became a hot-button issue, and I wanted to learn more. As I dug into the operations of how and why the USPS runs how it does, I'd learned a few things.

Did you know that the Postal Service receives NO tax dollars for operating expenses and relies on the sale of postage, products and services to fund its operations? Or that the Postal Service is the only organization in the country that has the resources, network infrastructure and logistical capability to regularly deliver to every residential and business address in the nation? These are things that make the USPS invaluable to every single American. This is why I decided to take this knowledge and turn it into something tangible.

With my design, I created a logo, art, apparel and a social media presence for the Save Our Stamps campaign. I sold custom apparel and donated 100% of the profits to purchase postage from the USPS and donated it to local Michigan businesses. I may only be only one person, but grassroots efforts like this are what drive real change. Even if I only educated or influenced one single person, the campaign was a success.

Facts derived from the USPS Postal Facts page. Read more at facts.usps.com and share this information with those around you. Knowledge is power.
